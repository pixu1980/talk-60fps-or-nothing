We are all great with someone else's browser

As long as a demo runs on the right laptop, every animation looks brilliant. The problem starts when the browser actually has to do its job.

Why should we understand it?

  • Users do not judge your code, they judge the pixels that reach the screen
  • They notice lag, input delay, choppy scroll, and layout instability
  • Understanding the renderer helps you choose better between CSS, JS, and asset strategy
